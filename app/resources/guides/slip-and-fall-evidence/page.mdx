## Preserve the scene quickly

Hazard conditions often change. Timely documentation can materially improve factual clarity. If you are also dealing with recorded statements or claim calls, our [insurance adjuster guide](/resources/guides/insurance-adjusters) covers the communication side.

## Build a clean evidence file

Organize documents so a timeline can be reviewed quickly and consistently.

### Core file categories

- Scene and condition photos.
- Witness contacts and summary notes.
- Incident documentation.
- Medical records and visit chronology.

## Communication strategy

Keep statements factual and concise across all channels.

### Do

- Confirm basics with consistency.
- Save all written communication.
- Keep one central timeline.

### Do not

- Speculate about fault conclusions.
- Discard shoes or clothing if relevant.
- Sign releases before review.

## Georgia note

Legal requirements can be fact-specific and time-sensitive. Verify current law and process requirements directly with counsel, and use our [medical documentation tips](/resources/guides/medical-documentation) to keep the injury timeline organized after the incident.
